## Story

A planet hurtling through space has had its core destabilized, threatening to implode and destroy the entire universe. BLOB (Bio-Logically Operated Being) is dispatched to the planet's surface to descend into its depths, locate 9 specific core components, and reassemble the planet's core before it's too late.

The core components needed are **randomized each playthrough** -- BLOB must first visit the core room to discover which 9 objects are required. Objects are scattered across the planet's 512-room underground network and some can only be obtained through trading at Cheops Pyramid exchange booths.

## Developer - Stephen Crow

Stephen Crow was a prominent ZX Spectrum programmer in the mid-1980s. Before Starquake, he created **Wizard's Lair** (1985), from which several Starquake mechanics evolved -- the elevator codes in Wizard's Lair became Starquake's teleporter system. 

Crow prototyped Starquake's mechanics in sandbox test screens, and the signature platform-laying mechanic emerged organically as a replacement for traditional jumping. He programmed the game entirely in Z80 assembly language for the 48K Spectrum, achieving remarkable technical feats including zero attribute clash (a notorious Spectrum limitation).

Crow also composed the C64 version's SID music himself.

## Reception and Awards

### Reviews
- **CRASH Magazine** (Issue 22): **96%** -- "no attribute clash at all," praised as a technical and gameplay masterpiece
- **CRASH Readers Awards**: Won **Best Sound FX** (remarkable for beeper-only Spectrum audio)
- **Your Sinclair**: Highly rated

### Awards
- **Golden Joystick Awards 1985**: Stephen Crow won **Best Programmer**
- **CRASH Awards 1986**: Stephen Crow won **Best Programmer**

### Legacy Rankings
- **Retro Gamer**: Ranked **10th** in Top 25 Spectrum Games

## Game at a Glance

- **512 flip-screen rooms** in a 16x32 grid
- **Open-world exploration** -- surface to deep underground
- **No jumping** -- build temporary crumbling platforms instead
- **9 randomized core pieces** to collect each playthrough
- **15 teleporter stations** with 5-letter codes for fast travel
- **Cheops Pyramid trading system** for item exchange
- **Hover pad vehicles** for flight sections
- **4-item FIFO inventory** system
- **4 lives**, 3 resource bars (energy, ammo, platforms)
