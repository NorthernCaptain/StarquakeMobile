# Starquake - Items and Trading

## Item Categories

### Core Pieces
- **9 core pieces** required to win the game
- Selected **randomly from the object pool** each playthrough
- Must be carried to the core room and deposited
- Some are found lying in rooms; others must be obtained through trading
- Each delivery scores **10,000 points**

### Access Card
The most important item in the game:
- **Universal key** -- opens any security door
- **Enables trading** at Cheops Pyramids (cannot trade without it)
- Should be obtained as early as possible and kept in inventory
- Lost if pushed out of FIFO inventory by picking up too many items

### Keys
- Basic door openers
- Used to unlock standard locked doors
- Single-use

### Numbered Chips
- Open **specific** security doors (each chip matches a specific door)
- Numbered to correspond to their target door

### Flexible Whatsit
- **One-time use** universal door opener
- Opens any door regardless of number
- Rare and valuable

### Resource Pickups (Instant Use)

These are consumed immediately on contact -- they don't go into inventory:

| Pickup | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Energy (small) | Restores small amount of energy |
| Energy (medium) | Restores moderate energy |
| Energy (large) | Restores large amount of energy |
| Ammunition (small) | Restores small amount of ammo |
| Ammunition (medium) | Restores moderate ammo |
| Ammunition (large) | Restores large amount of ammo |
| Platforms (small) | Restores small platform supply |
| Platforms (medium) | Restores moderate platform supply |
| Platforms (large) | Restores large platform supply |

### Multi-purpose Cylinder
- Special pickup that **tops up the lowest resource bar**
- If all three resources (energy, ammo, platforms) are full, grants an **extra life** instead
- Strategic: fill two resources before collecting to guarantee the third gets topped up, or fill all three for a free life

### Joystick Icons
- Collectible **extra life** pickups
- Grants one additional life on contact

## Cheops Pyramid Trading System

### How It Works
1. BLOB must have the **Access Card** in inventory
2. Approach a **Cheops Pyramid** structure (found in designated rooms throughout the map)
3. BLOB can **exchange** a non-core inventory item for a different item
4. The pyramid offers a specific item in exchange for what BLOB deposits
5. Many core pieces are **only obtainable** through pyramid trading

### Trading Strategy
- Always keep the Access Card -- it's required for all trades
- Some trades chain: Item A trades for Item B, which trades for a core piece
- With only 4 inventory slots (FIFO), planning trade routes is critical
- Visit the core first to know which 9 pieces are needed before trading blindly

### Inventory Management Tips
- **FIFO** means the oldest item drops when picking up a 5th
- Never accidentally push out the Access Card
- Plan collection routes to minimize backtracking
- Core pieces should be delivered to the core room as soon as possible to free inventory space

## Item Locations
- Item locations are **randomized each playthrough**
- The map structure is fixed, but objects are placed randomly
- The 9 required core pieces are randomly selected from the object pool
- This means every playthrough requires different exploration and trading strategies
